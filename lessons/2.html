Appreciating Arrays

The examples in the last lesson were written to allow you to understand how arrays work, but they don’t do much justice in terms of enhancing your appreciation of arrays. Let’s look at a few examples of how basic tasks work with and without arrays, this will also allow you to practice creating them yourself.

Utilising an array, calculate the average age of 25 of your peers at TEDI London.



C
int numStudents = 25; //number of students
double ages[25] = {18, 20, 34, 17, 19, 21, 22, 23, 26, 20, 22, 45, 15, 21, 22, 18, 20, 34, 17, 19, 21, 22, 23, 26, 20};
int sum = 0; //initialise sum
//iterate through array and sum numbers
for(int i = 0; i < numStudents; i++){
sum = sum + ages[i];
}
double mean = 0; //initialise mean
mean = sum/numStudents;
return(mean);
